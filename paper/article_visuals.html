— Experiments 7 & 8: What Breaks at 12B Scale —

Experiment 7: Where INT8 Quantization Breaks (and Where It's Lossless)

Weighted relative error by architectural component — GPT-OSS 20B (11.96B params, 459 tensors)

The finding: MoE expert blocks (79.9% of parameters) are lossless — already stored as mxfp4 integers. The real degradation lives in the attention output projections (25.7% error) and embedding layer (32.7% error). These components have wide dynamic ranges that per-tensor affine quantization can't handle.

Experiment 8: Error Propagation Through 24 Transformer Layers

Cosine similarity between original and quantized hidden states after each attention layer

Worst case: After 24 layers of quantized attention, hidden states drop to cosine 0.72. In practice, the 80/20 blend weight and expert immunity (85% lossless) dilute this significantly — but it's a real degradation that group-wise quantization would fix.

Experiment 8C: Router Sensitivity — The Good News

MoE expert selection agreement after INT8 quantization of router weights

The router is immune. Top-1 expert agreement is 100% across all layers. The MoE gating mechanism produces identical expert assignments from quantized snapshots. This is critical — router errors would cascade through the entire expert pathway.
